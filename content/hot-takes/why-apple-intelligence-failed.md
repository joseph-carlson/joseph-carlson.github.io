As all big tech companies are throwing everything they have into artificial intelligence research and development, the one company nearly everyone agrees is losing the race is Apple. Since the launch of their AI phone without AI to releasing a research paper claiming it's not their faults, a trend has started to emerge.

In this article, I'm going to lay out a few reasons why I believe the first trillion dollar company as missed the mark.

{{< img path="/hot-takes/liquid-ass.jpg" >}}

## 1 - Data

It's well known that the default settings of Apple devices send back less telemetry and user data to Cupertino than most others. While this is great for consumers, it's much less great when it comes to training artificial neural nets. 

Data is so valuable, in fact, there's an ongoing societal battle on whether these models should be trained on copyrighted material. And there is so much pressure that all the major players decided it would be easier to deal with the lawsuits than to catch up after artificial super intelligence has been reached.

## 2 - Compute

Data without compute doesn't allow shareholders to purchase their third yacht, so I'd day it's worth diving into this a little. 

When you consider who has the compute necessary to train these models, it's a fairly short list.

1. Nvidia - big surprise, only the most valuable company in the world
3. AMD, not as dev-friendly as Nvidia but not out of the race
2. Google, yeah, they have their TPUs
4. Intel, maybe? even the sinking ship rises above the tide
5. Apple with their M-series silicon

And for what it's worth, I love that Apple is trying to push their compute to the edge devices, especially with the OpenAIs NYT situation. But with that being said, in this business landscape, it's not the winning strategy. 

Advanced models require server farms to train, and from all appearances, they don't have the same infrastructure as others which have partnerships with Nvidia.

All home is not lost though, as Apple seems to be developing enterprise-level AI chips which have far more cores than the consumer line.

## 4 - Talent

If you're smart enough to be among the smartest AI researchers in the world, you're probably also smart enough to know which environment will give you the resources and autonomy to advance your work and which ones will not. 

This can be broken down into two parts:
1.  Many want to publish their research, and this is incompatible with Apple's consistently closed source standard business practices (except when discovering convenient truths).

Additionally, the engineers behind Apple's AI platform have not created the vast array of Developer-facing AI services. 

2. If you want to be the best, you want to be surrounded by the best. Throughout the last decade, PhDs and many other talented researchers have flocked to Google.
