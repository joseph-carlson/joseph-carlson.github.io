Slowly but surely, rights are being taken away. When you open your eyes, there's no denying that fact.

Now surely, there are many aspects of today's world where people are more free than in prior decades. 

Unfortunately, this website is about technology. In the world of tech, we are not free. Not free to repair. Not free to own. We didn't arrive here overnight, but rather over decades of increased regulation blocking new entrants and anticompetitive business practices by the leadership of Big Tech organizations, all while the FTC hid away in the closet with a blindfold on (for the most part).

Disclaimer: do not misunderstand my words. I am all for making a profit by providing a product or service. I don't by any means propose top-down control of the market would have a more desirable outcome. Capitalism with limited government regulation is the best we have - and with that we must tread carefully to stay on the rails of progress.

Companies say it's for your own good. They don't want you to hurt yourself. They want to ensure the highest level of quality for the product they have provided, during all stages of ownership.
