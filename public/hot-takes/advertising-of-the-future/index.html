 / Advertising of the Future

As technology evolves, platforms do as well. We’ve gone through word-of-mouth, newspaper segments, static website promotions and personalized advertisements tailored to your personal search history. Just when you thoughts tailored advertisements couldn’t get any worse, as they are being inserted into every free second of unallocated thought, you would be wrong.

There have been a few recent instances of excessive and intrusive advertisement practices that have pushed me back to good old paperback books in recent months.

  1. Amazon Prime Video ad placement during pause
  2. YouTube in-video promotions after YouTube Premium membership

Everything comes down to monetization. There’s a reason why the “Accept All” button for cookies while browsing websites is one click, yet it takes several more to reject each non-essential category.

There’s a degree of fatigue walking through the world knowing nearly the entire economy is driven by a few key corporations that accumulated enough market share where corporate goals have shifted to maximum value extraction at all costs necessary, laws and regulations be damned. Now I’m all for innovation and reaping the rewards of providing a useful product to the masses, but I can’t truthfully say that is what most of these corporations are doing. Now that they have the capital requires, there has been a shift to purchasing up and coming competitors before they even have a chance to challenge the existing oligopoly.

Now onto the reason I’m writing this article to begin with, and how the situation above ties into everything.

It’s my belief that the training data of large language models will become the new advertisement platform. This reality struck me once I realized how many of those around me have have stopped searching websites in favor of GPT platforms. Google leading the page with their AI summary responding to your query seems like just about as slippery as slopes get. Soon enough, most people won’t even go to the primary resources in favor of AI summaries interpreting those resources.

In my eyes, this presents the same situation as the Amazon Marketplace promoting their Amazon Basics products, as well as the other more inconspicuously named store brands.
