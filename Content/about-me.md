# About Me

I can go through the formal education and limited work experience I have in IT. An associate’s degree, a few jobs, some certifications, all supposedly leading indicators of a successful career. My opinion, they’re nice to have, but people rely too much on institutions approving and certifying the competence of certain domains. A piece of paper that says, “you got a chance at being able to do this job” or “maybe you won’t lose this company money”. Maybe it’s just a statement that you can commit yourself to a goal and stick with it for four years in the case of the oh-so-revered Bachelor’s degree.

I think they’re all good things, and I have nothing against those who dedicate themselves to acquiring knowledge in an institution that organizes the curriculum, stages the material to prevent overwhelm, maybe even provide several years of focus without the impedance of working a full-time job. I sure hope the person operating in open-heart surgery or engineering the building I am sitting in had some formal education. Heck, I have a few letters next to my name and I’ve only been in the game a couple of years. I just think people should consider factors beyond B.S. on a resume for a more all-inclusive evaluation. 

This revised “About Me” section will be the first of many changes to come on this website. First and foremost, the articles and documentation that will be added are first and foremost to help me synthesize and better understand what I’m learning. In the process of explaining a concept or an event, it brings up questions I don’t even think to ask. It’s those inquiries and rabbit holes that direct my investigation to better understanding the technology of the modern day and the implications if we don’t do our due diligence as security professionals. If the readers of this website happen to glean some insights from my posts, that would certainly be a neat consequence of this side project.
